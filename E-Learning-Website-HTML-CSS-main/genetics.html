Genetics

Taught by experienced experts, this course explores the science of heredity and variation in living organisms. Students learn how traits are inherited, how genes function, and how genetic technologies are applied in medicine, agriculture, and research. The program combines theory with practical lab work, preparing learners for careers in biotechnology, healthcare, forensic science, and academic research.

4.14 3.3L+ Learners Intermediate

About this Course

  1. Introduction to Genetics – History, scope, and significance.
  2. Mendelian Genetics – Laws of inheritance and trait prediction.
  3. Molecular Genetics – DNA, RNA, and protein synthesis.
  4. Chromosome Structure & Function – Karyotyping, chromosomal abnormalities.
  5. Gene Expression & Regulation – How genes are turned on/off.
  6. Genetic Variation & Mutation – Causes, types, and effects.
  7. Population & Evolutionary Genetics – Gene pools, genetic drift, and selection.
  8. Human Genetics – Genetic disorders and counseling.
  9. Applied Genetics – Agriculture, medicine, and forensic applications.
  10. Final Project / Research Work – Genetic analysis or case study.
